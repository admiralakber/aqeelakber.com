Emerging Technology — a conversation with Mei Weng Brough-Smyth at the Knox Cafe in Watson, with cafe staff and Esther saying hi. The Aqeel Akber Extra Extra Extravaganza, made by Aqeel in Canberra.

Watch the full episode below — also on the podcast feed wherever you listen.

In this episode

  • At the Knox Cafe in Watson, Mei Weng Brough-Smyth joins a table-side chat that opens with a deceptively simple question: do you actually do what's good for you?
  • Everyone admits to running on some internal prioritisation algorithm — the tension is whether you can still trust that compass when life gets noisy.
  • Meos surfaces not as a demo, but as a provocation: what if software were built around you as an operating system, not around keeping you engaged?
  • The conversation keeps circling hijacked attention — the scroll that used to feel fun, the blockers people reach for, and the uneasy metaphor of a digital cigarette.
  • Deleting social media sounds both sensible and absurd at once, as if community glue and engineered drain might be the same problem wearing different masks.
  • Before the coffees go cold, the wander turns technical — tool calling, what an AI-native kernel might owe you, and why privacy keeps re-entering the room.

The conversation goes further than a list can — watch or listen above.
